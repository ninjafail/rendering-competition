Welcome to our submission to the Rendering Competition!

From idea to reality

When brainstorming about what to create for the topic 'Chaos in Harmony', we discovered a fun and interesting interpretation. As a programmer one could argue we might have created the most harmonious environment: A clean and tidy desk with an impressive setup.

How could this be chaotic? Well, take a closer look at the details in the scene and you will see that there are many small disarrangements. We were inspired by pictures that come up when you google: 'pictures that annoy your inner perfectionist'. They represent the 'Chaos in Harmony'. If you want you can look for them yourself. Can you find all 7 dissarrangements?

If you want to find them without help, stop reading now. All disarrangements will be revealed on the final page. Now, we give you the first one for free and a few hints: The tea pot is customly modeled after the Masochist's teapot from the cover of Don Norman's 'The Design of Everyday Things'. We also customly modeled the coasters, the mouse pad, and the mouse cable. Further, the screen and the textbook use custom textures.

Furthermore, we added a few small easter eggs into the scene. On the top right of our screen you can see the very first sketch of our scene. Below it there one of the first rendered images of the scene, giving a scene-ception effect. Placed on the left of the monitor there is a little paperweight/sculpture of the blender mascot Suzanne.
